## Before you start

All you need is a Google account. Ledger handles everything else — no setup or configuration required.

## The sheet selector

After signing in you land on the sheet selector. From here you can create a new budget sheet — enter a name and click Create. Ledger writes the full Config template and the current month tab, then opens the dashboard.

## What gets created

A new sheet includes:

| Tab | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Config | Stores your default income, categories, cards, fixed expenses, and saving goals |
| Current month (e.g. "Jun 2026") | Pre-populated with your income and fixed expenses; ready for transactions |

The Config tab is organized into labeled sections separated by blank rows. The parser locates each section by its exact uppercase header (`INCOME`, `CATEGORIES`, `CARDS`, `FIXED EXPENSES`, `SAVING GOALS`).

## Switching sheets

Open the sheet selector at any time from the header. Selecting a different sheet immediately switches the dashboard to that sheet's data. Your last-used sheet is remembered across page reloads. You can only switch between sheets that were created through Ledger.

> **Tip:** You can create more than one budget sheet through Ledger — one for personal spending and one for a shared household budget, for example. Switch between them from the sheet selector in the header.
