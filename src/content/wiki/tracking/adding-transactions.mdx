## The Transactions tab

The Transactions tab shows all entries for the currently selected month, sorted by date. A month picker at the top lets you browse any past month. The add button opens the entry form.

## Fields in detail

### Date
Defaults to today's date. Change it to record a transaction that happened on a different day. Month tabs are created on demand — if you enter a date in a month that has no tab yet, Ledger creates one automatically with your current income and fixed-expense defaults.

### Amount
A plain number. No currency symbol. Decimals are supported (`14.50`). Enter a negative number to record a refund — it will reduce your spending total for that category.

### Category
A dropdown of the categories you set up in Config. Categories group your spending for the breakdown view and savings goal tracking. If no category fits yet, open Config → Categories to add one — it appears in the dropdown immediately.

### Card / payment method
A dropdown of the payment methods from Config → Cards. Useful for tracking which card you used, separating debit from credit, or distinguishing cash from digital payments.

### Note
Optional free text. This field is full-text searchable from the transaction list. Good uses: merchant name, a reminder ("split with Alex"), or a receipt reference.

## Searching and filtering

The transaction list has a search bar and filter chips above it. Search matches against category, card, and note text. The filter chips let you narrow to a specific category or card. Sort by date (newest first by default) or by amount.

## Deleting a transaction

Tap the delete icon on any row to remove it. The row is removed from your Google Sheet immediately.

## Editing a transaction

Tap the edit icon on any row to open an inline edit form pre-filled with the current values. Change what you need and save — the row in your sheet is updated in place.

## Past months

You can still add and delete transactions in past months — useful for recording something you missed. You can also update a past month's income and fixed-expense amounts from the monthly config tab in the **Everything** tab. Adding a transaction to a past month may recalculate carry-over amounts for all subsequent months.

> **Tip:** The transaction list total shown at the top of the tab excludes carry-over entries. That number represents only the spending you actually entered for the month.
