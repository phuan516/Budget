## Your sheet is the backup

Because Ledger writes everything to a Google Sheet you own, your data is automatically backed up to Google Drive. Google Drive keeps a revision history — you can restore any earlier version of your sheet from **File → Version history** in Google Sheets.

## Exporting to CSV or Excel

Google Sheets can export any sheet tab directly:

1. Open your budget sheet in Google Sheets.
2. Select the tab you want to export (e.g. "Jun 2026" or "Config").
3. Go to **File → Download** and choose **.csv** or **.xlsx**.

To export all months at once, use **File → Download → .xlsx** — this downloads a single Excel file with one worksheet per tab.

## Downloading all data at once

From Google Drive:

1. Right-click your budget spreadsheet.
2. Click **Download**.

Google will convert it to `.xlsx` format with all tabs preserved.

## Sharing the sheet with someone else

If you want to share your budget with a partner, accountant, or advisor:

1. Open the sheet in Google Sheets.
2. Click **Share** in the top-right corner.
3. Add the person's email and set their permission level (Viewer, Commenter, or Editor).

The shared person will see the live data and any updates you make in Ledger. They can also edit directly in Google Sheets if you grant Editor access.

## Moving to a different Google account

1. Open the sheet in Google Sheets under your current account.
2. Go to **File → Make a copy** to create a copy in the target account's Drive (you will need to be signed into both accounts, or share the sheet with the new account first).
3. Sign into Ledger with the new account and create a new sheet, then manually copy your data from the old sheet into the new one.

## What is not in the sheet

The only thing not stored in your Google Sheet is your Ledger authentication session — that lives in a secure cookie in your browser. It expires and is re-created each time you sign in. There is nothing to export or migrate from Ledger's servers.

> **Tip:** For a read-only year-end archive, download the full `.xlsx` at the end of December. The sheet includes every transaction and all your config defaults for that year.
