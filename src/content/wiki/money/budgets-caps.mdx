## Fixed expenses

Fixed expenses are your recurring monthly bills — rent, subscriptions, insurance, phone plan. They are defined in **Config → Fixed Expenses** and show on the Overview tab as a separate section from your variable (category-based) spending.

### Adding a fixed expense

In Config → Fixed Expenses, enter a name and a monthly amount. You can also enter a percentage of income instead of a dollar amount — the two fields stay in sync as you type. Click Add.

The new expense is synced to the current month's tab and all future month tabs. Past month tabs are not changed.

### Per-month overrides

On the Overview tab, each fixed expense row has an edit icon. Tap it to set a different amount for that specific month — useful when a bill changes for one month (a one-time annual payment, a promo rate expiring, etc.). To reset a month's override back to the Config default, clear the override and save.

### Fixed expenses in the Everything tab

The **Everything** tab includes a **Fixed Expenses Over Time** chart showing each fixed expense's amount per month across your full history. This makes it easy to see when amounts changed due to overrides.

## Saving goals

Saving goals track progress toward a financial target. Define them in **Config → Saving Goals** with a name, a target amount, and an optional starting balance.

### Linking a goal to categories

A saving goal tracks spending in specific categories as "money saved." For example, a "Vacation" goal linked to a "Travel" category will sum all Travel transactions and show that total as progress toward the goal's target.

Progress is shown as a percentage bar on the Overview tab.

### Allocating a leftover surplus

When a past month ends with unspent budget, a banner appears in the Transactions tab offering to allocate that surplus to a saving goal. One click records the allocation and updates the goal's progress.

## How fixed expenses affect carry-over

At month-end, Ledger calculates: income − variable spending − fixed expenses = surplus or deficit. If the result is negative (deficit), the difference is written as a carry-over entry into the next month. Fixed expenses count toward this calculation.

> **Tip:** Use the percentage-of-income field for expenses that should scale with your income — for example, a savings contribution target set at 20% of income. When your income changes month to month, the target adjusts automatically.
